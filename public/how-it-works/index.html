How the Property Counter-Offer Calculator Works

A simple process for comparing an asking price with local market context and the cost of work required.

Calculator About How It Works FAQ Privacy Terms

Step 1: Check the local market picture

The calculator lets you compare the property against HM Land Registry averages for the selected area and property type. This gives you a market anchor before you decide whether the asking price looks fair, stretched, or conservative.

Step 2: Add the asking price

Enter the seller's asking price or guide price. The tool uses that number as the starting point for your review so you can see how much of the price may need to be adjusted once condition issues are considered.

Step 3: Record repair and improvement costs

Select the visible issues that matter to you, from structural concerns and dated systems to cosmetic work and garden issues. Each item contributes to the estimated cost of bringing the property closer to your expectations.

Step 4: Review the counter-offer output

As you add items, the calculator updates the suggested counter-offer figure. This gives you a fast way to build a more evidence-based opening position for negotiation.

Best way to use the result

Treat the result as a negotiation framework rather than a guaranteed valuation. Pair it with survey findings, comparable listings, and the competitiveness of the local market before deciding what you are willing to offer.
