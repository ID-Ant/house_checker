How much below asking price should I offer on a house in the UK?

There is no fixed percentage that works for every property. A more reliable approach is to compare the asking price with local sold-price evidence, assess the condition of the property, and account for how much work is needed before choosing your opening offer.

Is the calculator a formal property valuation?

No. It is a decision-support tool for buyers. It helps you review market context and likely repair costs, but it does not replace a survey, lender valuation, or specialist advice.

Where does the market data come from?

The tool uses HM Land Registry data and related local market context to help buyers compare an asking price against recent area-level information.

Should I offer exactly what the calculator shows?

Not necessarily. The figure is a useful negotiation starting point. You still need to consider local demand, competing buyers, the survey outcome, and how strongly you want the property.

Can I adjust repair costs?

Yes. The calculator starts with preset costs, but you can amend them to better fit the property's condition or the quotes you expect to receive.

Who is this most useful for?

It is especially useful for first-time buyers, buyers comparing several properties, and anyone preparing a counter-offer on a home that needs work.

Can sold-price data justify a lower offer?

Yes. Recent sold-price evidence from similar nearby properties can give buyers a stronger basis for explaining why an asking price looks high or why a lower offer is reasonable.
